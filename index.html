What's inside

A whole toolkit, already in your pocket.

Everything below is live in the app today — built one real, useful thing at a time.

Log around real food

Search 500+ Indian regional dishes with real carb estimates, scan a barcode for packaged food, or log by voice in your own language.

Glucose, HbA1c & BP

Track blood glucose, HbA1c and blood pressure together, with trend charts and a clear in-range view — the full picture, not just one number.

Snap your lab report New

Photograph a printed lab report and the app reads it on-device — HbA1c, glucose, cholesterol, kidney markers and more land in an editable review you confirm before saving. Nothing leaves your phone.

Reports your doctor can read

One tap turns everything into a clean PDF or CSV — and “Explain My Report” adds a plain-language summary of your own data, so it makes sense before you even sit down.

Reminders & gentle streaks

Medication reminders and daily goals for glucose, food, hydration and activity — with forgiving streaks that don't punish a single missed day.

Understand your patterns Premium

Pattern Insights reflect how your meals and routines connect to your glucose over time — drawn from your own history, never guesswork or predictions.

Caregiver & family sharing Premium

Share a read-only view of your latest readings and medications with a family member or caregiver, with an invite code you can revoke any time.

Complete care, not just glucose

An emergency medical ID for first responders, foot-health checks, preventive-screening reminders, ketones and a sick-day mode — the parts of diabetes care most apps skip.

Buy Supplies, near you

A supplies tab that detects your country and will surface local sellers for test strips, sensors and monitors — checkout always happens on the seller's own site.

Coming next: family multi-profiles, more home cuisines, Dexcom CGM readings, and an iOS build.

Why we're building this

The food deserved better.

Most diabetes apps were built around a Western pantry — cereal, sandwiches, pasta — and treat everything else as an edge case. If you grew up on dal, adobo, pho, or shawarma, you already know what that's like: logging your actual dinner and getting a shrug instead of a real number.

MyHealthyGlucose started with one region done properly — over 500 Indian regional dishes, real carb estimates, real cultural context — instead of a thousand cuisines done shallowly. Everything we build is about doing that same thing for more of the world, one cuisine at a time, without cutting corners to get there faster.

— The MyHealthyGlucose team, DataSpark Tech LLC
